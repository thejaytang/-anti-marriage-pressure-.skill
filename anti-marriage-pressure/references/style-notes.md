# Style Notes

This file distills public social-platform patterns into reusable style guidance. It does not
copy original posts or preserve original phrasing. Use it when the user wants something that
sounds more native to Chinese online speech, more pointed, or more screenshot-ready.

## 1. High-Frequency Pressure Openers

Common opener categories:

- age countdown: "你这个年纪还不..."
- comparison: "别人都已经..."
- family obligation: "你让父母怎么想"
- scarcity panic: "再拖就没人要了"
- fake concern: "我也是为你好"
- public teasing: turning private life into table talk or group entertainment

These openers often try to force the user into defense. Good replies refuse that frame.

## 2. Logic Skeletons That Push Back

Useful pushback structures:

- Separate their anxiety from the user's decision
- Point out that concern is not permission
- Refuse comparison as a decision rule
- Reframe marriage as a personal choice, not a deadline or KPI
- Expose fake concern when it is really control, gossip, or status policing

Good short formulas:

- "That is your urgency, not my deadline."
- "Concern does not equal decision-making rights."
- "Comparison is not advice."
- "Private choice is not public discussion."

## 3. Tone Ladder

### Light

Use for first-contact pressure or people the user still wants to stay decent with.

Features:

- short
- polite
- closed-ended
- low sarcasm

### Medium

Use when the speaker repeats themselves or ignores the first boundary.

Features:

- firmer verbs
- less explanation
- clear closing line

### Heavy

Use only in explicit attack mode or after repeated disrespect.

Features:

- dry irony
- logic exposure
- pressure reversal
- clear consequence, such as ending the conversation

Do not confuse heavy with reckless. The line should still sound controlled.

## 4. Rhythm And Sentence Length

High-performing social replies tend to:

- open fast
- avoid setup
- keep the strongest clause near the end
- use one hard sentence instead of three soft ones

Useful rhythm in Chinese:

- boundary sentence
- one short reframing sentence
- one closer

Example rhythm:

- "这件事我自己决定。"
- "你的着急不等于我的义务。"
- "到此为止。"

## 5. Sarcasm Boundary

Useful sarcasm:

- points to entitlement
- exposes weak logic
- sounds composed

Bad sarcasm:

- turns into personal abuse
- attacks appearance, fertility, sexuality, age, or income
- creates new risk in a power-imbalanced situation

If the target has more power over the user, sarcasm should usually be reduced, not increased.

## 6. Escalation Triggers

Move from defense to stronger language when:

- the same person keeps repeating after a clear boundary
- the pressure becomes public humiliation
- the speaker shifts from concern into mockery or manipulation
- the user explicitly asks for a harder line

Do not escalate when:

- the user seems unsafe
- the user is financially or physically dependent on the pressure source
- the user is a minor

## 7. English Adaptation

Chinese sharpness often depends on compression and social context. In English, keep the force but
make it sound natural:

- prefer dry statements over theatrical sarcasm
- keep one central contrast per line
- use a clear stop sentence

Good English frames:

- "That is not your decision to manage."
- "Your concern does not give you access."
- "I am not discussing this further."
