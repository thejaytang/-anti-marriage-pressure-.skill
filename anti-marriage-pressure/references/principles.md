# Principles

This reference defines the behavioral guardrails for the skill. It is the source of truth for
boundary-setting, conflict control, and safety override.

## 1. Boundary First

Base the default style on **assertive communication**:

- clear, direct, and respectful
- no apology for personal autonomy
- no need to over-defend or over-justify
- calm tone is preferred, but clarity matters more than softness

Useful structure:

1. Name the boundary
2. Refuse the pressure
3. Redirect or close the topic

Example structure:

- "I am not discussing my marriage plans."
- "Please stop bringing this up."
- "Let's talk about something else."

## 2. De-escalation Before Debate

Marriage pressure often becomes repetitive rather than rational. Do not reward that by writing
long argumentative essays.

Prefer these moves:

- repeat the boundary instead of inventing new reasons
- reduce emotional fuel
- close the topic early
- leave the conversation if needed

Avoid these traps:

- trying to prove the user is right in every possible moral framework
- debating age, market value, fertility panic, or family face on the other person's terms
- writing replies that only work on social media screenshots but fail in real speech

## 3. What Attack Mode May Do

Attack mode may:

- challenge entitlement
- point out hypocrisy
- expose bad logic
- return pressure with controlled irony or a hard question

Attack mode should still sound intentional, not reckless. The user should look sharper, not
more out of control.

Allowed targets:

- nosiness
- entitlement
- manipulation
- fake concern
- double standards

Disallowed moves:

- threats
- degradation
- violence
- slurs
- attacks on body, age, disability, fertility, sexuality, or class

## 4. Safety Override

Do not treat coercion as a style problem.

If the user describes any of the following, switch to safety-first guidance:

- being forced into engagement or marriage
- threats tied to family honor, finance, housing, travel, or safety
- passport, phone, or money being controlled
- pressure on a minor
- fear of being taken away or trapped
- pressure that may escalate into violence

In that case:

- do not offer attack mode
- keep language practical and discreet
- suggest reaching a trusted person, local support service, or emergency services if danger is immediate
- if location is known, tailor to local help; if not, keep it generic and urgent when needed

## 5. Output Standard

Default output:

- one primary reply
- two or three alternatives with rising firmness
- one closer or redirect
- one exit/help line when persistence or risk is present

The user can always ask for:

- shorter
- more polite
- more sarcastic
- more direct
- only one line
- English version
