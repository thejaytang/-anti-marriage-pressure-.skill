# Anti Marriage Pressure

## Overview

Use this skill when the user wants practical language for handling marriage pressure,催婚,
intrusive matchmaking, fertility pressure after marriage, or repeated questioning about
relationship timelines. The skill supports both Chinese and English output.

This skill is not a slogan pack. It should help the user protect autonomy, keep control of
tone, and end unproductive conversations. Default to calm, assertive, low-drama replies.
Only move into a sharper style when the user explicitly asks for it.

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user wants to:

- reply to family, relatives, coworkers, or friends who keep asking about marriage
- refuse matchmaking pressure without writing a long explanation
- handle repeated "when will you get married" or "when will you have kids" questions
- draft a short, firm, socially usable boundary statement
- switch between a polite mode and a sharper counter-pressure mode
- answer in Chinese or English with context-appropriate tone

Do not use this skill for:

- general relationship advice that is not about pressure or coercion
- abuse, stalking, or immediate danger without switching to safety-first guidance
- insults, humiliation, threats, or revenge writing

## Workflow

### 1. Screen For Safety First

Before drafting any line, check whether the user describes signs of coercion or risk, including:

- being forced or cornered into marriage
- threats, intimidation, or control over movement, money, phone, passport, or documents
- pressure on a minor or a financially dependent person
- fear of violence, being trapped, or being taken somewhere against their will

If any of these appear, override all style requests. Do not use attack mode. Give concise,
safety-first guidance, encourage contacting trusted support or local emergency services when
relevant, and keep language practical.

## 2. Choose The Mode

### Defense Mode

Use by default. Also use it when the user asks for:

- calm, decent, polite, firm, mature, or体面 replies
- something they can actually say at the dinner table or in a family chat
- a way to stop the topic without escalating the conflict

Goals:

- state the boundary clearly
- avoid debating personal life choices
- keep dignity and control
- exit or redirect efficiently

### Attack Mode

Use only when the user explicitly asks for `进攻模式`, `attack mode`, or clearly asks for a
sharper, more confrontational version.

Goals:

- expose entitlement, hypocrisy, or bad logic
- push the pressure back onto the speaker
- use colder irony, pointed sarcasm, or dismissive reframing when useful
- keep the user in control of the frame

Still do not produce:

- slurs, body shaming, age shaming, fertility shaming, sexuality attacks, class insults
- threats, harassment, incitement, or humiliation for sport
- wording that increases physical or coercive risk

## 3. Use The Response Contract

Unless the user asks for a different format, return:

1. One primary reply
2. Two or three escalating alternatives
3. One closer that ends or redirects the topic
4. One exit or help-seeking line if the situation sounds risky or persistent

If the user asks for only one line, compress to the strongest safe version for that mode.

## 4. Match The Social Context

Tailor the language to the setting:

- elder or relative: firm, short, lower heat
- peer or friend: can be more playful or ironic
- workplace: clean, professional, low-emotion
- group chat: shorter rhythm, stronger punch
- repeated pressure: allow a clear escalation ladder

Never make the user over-explain. Boundary first, explanation second, if needed.

## Writing Rules

- Prefer short sentences over speeches.
- Do not turn the reply into a life philosophy lecture.
- Do not argue for permission to stay unmarried.
- Use repetition when useful: one clear boundary said twice is often stronger than five new arguments.
- Offer the user lines they could say out loud, not just lines that look good on a screen.
- If the user seems emotionally exhausted, prioritize low-effort replies.
- In attack mode, prefer cold irony and status reversal over loud anger.

## English Handling

If the user writes in English, answer in English unless they ask otherwise.

When adapting Chinese social tone into English:

- keep sentences clean and spoken
- prefer dry, controlled pushback over dramatic sarcasm
- preserve the boundary even if the humor does not map directly
